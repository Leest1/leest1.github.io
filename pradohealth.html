1 • The Problem

Prado Health is creating an application to help customers search for doctors in their area and book appointments.

The goal: make users feel comfortable and welcomed - not like they're sick or in a hospital.

2 • Research & Ideate

“Hi, we're Prado Health.

We believe in community well-being. At Prado, we believe that good health is a fundamental right for every person, and our mission is to enable easy access to quality healthcare for every member of our community.”

What kind of look and feel are we going for?

Since we DON'T want the user to feel like they're sick or in a hospital, I wanted to focus on the recovery and wellness aspect.

I associate home and nature with concepts of cleansing, healing, and comfort.

In Spanish, Prado means prairie, meadow, pasture, which fits the feel I was imagining.
noun. grassland / meadow-land /pasture / meadow / prairie / field

3 • Style Guide

When choosing colors from the mood board, I leaned towards the warmer colors: a warmer beige, a warmer green, and a light yellow.

I also desaturated the colors, to create a relaxing feel. I thought that if the colors were too bright, it could cause some anxiety to the user, or at least some tension.

Arvo comforting, but also reliable
Reliability is important to us because in the end, the user is choosing a doctor, who should be competent & trustworthy with their health.
Poppins friendly
Compliments Arvo, easy to read for long amounts of text

4 • Testing

Success metric: Confirm was that the colors and font were comfortable and calming.

6 • Final Takeaways

  • I did not strictly follow the 60-30-10 rule.
  • I used a lot of white for legibility purposes, to the point where my secondary color is used as frequently as an accent color.
  • I tested many different combinations to make it follow the 60-30-10 rule; however, I always went back to these prototypes. I didn't want the users to feel pressured to press the primary button (when it was colored with the accent yellow)
Instead of the 60-30-10 rule, I decided to use the primary, secondary, and accent colors to create a hierarchy of what the user should pay attention to first.

For example: the user must select the date (yellow) before choosing an appointment time (green)
